Morrocan Couscous

Vegetables marrocan couscous

This is a light, fresh and easy meal, quick to make and goes along very well with chicken and sour cream. It oozes summer vibes.

Ingridients

  • Couscous - 500 grams
  • Carrot - 1 large size
  • Leek - 1 large stem
  • Onion - 1 midium size
  • Garlic - 5 large cloves
  • Sicilian lemon - 1 large size
  • Cashew or Brazil nut - 100 grams
  • Parsiley - to taste
  • Mint - to taste
  • Butter - to taste
  • Olive oil - to taste
  • Sal - to taste
  • Black pepper - to taste
  • Chicken or Vegetables broth - enough to cover the couscous

Steps

  1. The time to cook this meal is greatly reduced if you deal with the choping first. So finely chop the garlic, onion, parseley and mint, the carrot, leek and nuts can be choped to your liking, now reserve;
  2. Heat the broth (can be substituted by water), no need to boil. Place the couscous in a bowl, add just enough broth to covers the couscous, lid it and leave it for about 4 minutes. Remove the lid, loosen the couscous and reserve;
  3. Tost the nuts in a frying pan, just'til they release their oils and became fragrant, reserve;
  4. In the same frying pan add just enough olive oil to fry and soften the onion and garlic;
  5. Once they are fragrant and soften add the leek and carrot with the butter, saute until the butter melts and incorporete nicely, lightly season with salt and black pepper;
  6. It's assembly time, in the bowl with the couscous add the vegetables and a generous amount of olive oil. Add the zests and the juice of the sicilian lemon to taste;
  7. Add the choped parseley, mint and nuts, mix all toghter and adjust the seasoning with salt and black pepper. Now it's done, serve it and enjoy.
